Biography

Dr. Anirach Mingkhwan is an Associate Professor at King Mongkut's University of Technology North Bangkok (KMUTNB). He holds a Ph.D. from Liverpool John Moores University, UK. A dedicated researcher and educator, his work is centered on the intersection of artificial intelligence, computer networks, and big data. Dr. Mingkhwan has a proven track record of academic leadership, having served as the Dean of the Faculty of Industrial Technology and Management at KMUTNB from 2012 to 2020, where he was instrumental in advancing curriculum development and research initiatives. His contributions to the field are aimed at bridging academic theory with real-world applications to meet both industry and societal demands.

Current Projects

Natural Inspired Computing

An ongoing project exploring the application of nature-inspired algorithms to solve complex computational problems. This research focuses on developing efficient algorithms inspired by biological systems and natural processes.

AI-Powered Libraries

Research on transforming traditional libraries into AI-powered knowledge management systems. This project investigates how artificial intelligence can enhance information retrieval, user experience, and knowledge organization in library environments.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation Systems

Development of enhanced text representation methods for improved information retrieval. This work focuses on improving the accuracy and efficiency of text-based information systems using advanced AI techniques.

Network Forensic Analysis

Advanced research in traffic flow classification and visualization for network security. This project develops tools and methodologies for detecting and analyzing network anomalies and security threats.

Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

Dr. Mingkhwan is committed to fostering a learning environment that encourages critical thinking and practical application of knowledge. He has a particular interest in how different learning styles, such as the VARK model, can be accommodated in blended learning environments to enhance student achievement. His approach to education is informed by his research into adaptive learning systems and the integration of technology in the classroom.
